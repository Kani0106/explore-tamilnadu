Culture, Heritage & Warm Hospitality

Tamil Nadu is one of India’s oldest living cultures, home to classical Tamil language, Bharatanatyam dance and Carnatic music.

“The past lives here — in temple corridors, quiet libraries, music halls and kitchens.”

The state’s coastline nurtures fishing hamlets and ports; the Western Ghats protect tea gardens and wildlife. Cities like Chennai and Coimbatore power industry and arts.

Cuisine

Idli, dosa, sambar, filter coffee and fiery Chettinad flavours. Banana-leaf meals are a delicious ritual.

Festivals

Pongal harvest celebrations, Chithirai festival in Madurai, and Chennai’s Margazhi Music Season.

Arts

Silk weaving in Kanchipuram, bronze casting in Swamimalai, vibrant kolams decorating doorsteps.

Plan by Region

Chennai, Mamallapuram and Rameswaram mix beaches, seafood and sacred sites.
Thanjavur, Trichy and Madurai are dotted with grand Dravidian temples and riverside towns.
Ooty, Kodaikanal and Valparai offer cool weather, tea estates and trekking.
